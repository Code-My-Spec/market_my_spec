# Agency Owner Alex

> "I have 90 days to make a client money or I work for free. Strategy speed is not a preference — it's a revenue-protection function."

Proto-persona for Market My Spec's agency customer tier. Built primarily from a direct PM interview with the first real customer (Growth Tech Marketing) and triangulated against agency onboarding research and performance-guarantee model studies. Mark as proto: validate against 3+ additional agency owner interviews before treating any claim as load-bearing.

## Role

Owner of a marketing or creative agency. Team is small; client management decisions and tooling decisions are made solely by the owner — no procurement committee, no CTO approvals, no legal review for tools under a monthly budget threshold. Semi-technical: has shipped products and can operate Claude Code, but the business is marketing-first, not engineering-first.

Manages a portfolio of client accounts, each under a performance or value guarantee that creates hard deadlines for demonstrating results. Currently serving event venue clients; actively expanding into other verticals. [E1, E2]

## Goals

**Protect revenue under the guarantee model.** The agency offers clients a 90-day performance guarantee — if no measurable revenue results in 90 days, the client gets the work for free. Speed to strategy is the primary lever: the faster a grounded strategy is produced and executed, the lower the risk of fulfilling the guarantee at cost. [E1, E2]

**Scale strategy delivery without scaling headcount.** Agency onboarding is labor-intensive — strategy development, positioning, ICP definition, and channel planning can consume 15–40 hours per new client engagement. AI-assisted strategy creation compresses this without adding staff. [E3]

**Create a repeatable strategic process across clients.** Each new client vertical requires a fresh strategy. A structured, interview-driven process that produces consistent artifacts (positioning, ICP, 90-day plan) makes it possible to apply the same methodology across event venues, restaurants, retail, and beyond. [E1, E3]

## Pain Points

**Strategy creation is the most labor-intensive phase of onboarding.** The first 30 days of a client engagement are strategy-heavy and billable only in aggregate — if strategy takes 3 weeks instead of 3 days, the 90-day clock is burning before execution begins. [E2, E3]

**Generic AI output doesn't meet client expectations.** Asking ChatGPT to "write a marketing strategy for an event venue" produces generic, surface-level output that wouldn't survive a client review. A structured interview that pulls the client's actual context before generating artifacts is the difference between an agency-grade deliverable and generic AI slop. [E1]

**Expanding verticals means starting from scratch each time.** The agency's current depth is event venues. Each new vertical requires new ICP assumptions, new positioning, new channel logic. A tool that walks through structured research and produces grounded artifacts gives the agency a methodology that travels across verticals. [E1]

**Mix of tools, no unified strategy process.** Current workflow is a mix of tools with no single system for strategy development. Strategy lives in Notion pages, slide decks, and emails — hard to templatize, hard to reuse. [E1]

## Context

**Semi-technical founder who can operate agentic tools.** Has built software products and is comfortable using Claude Code. The barrier is not technical ability — it's whether a tool fits into a marketing agency's workflow rather than a software team's workflow. [E1]

**Performance-guarantee model is an emerging agency differentiator.** Month-to-month contracts and revenue-tied guarantees are an accelerating trend in agency pricing as of 2026. Agencies using this model compete on speed and accountability rather than deliverable volume. [E2]

**Solo decision-maker for tooling.** For tools in the $50–$500/month range, the agency owner makes the call alone and same-day. No procurement process, no approval chain. Evaluation is personal: "did it produce something I'd send to a client?" [E1, E2]

**Client base: event venues, expanding.** Current ICP is B2B/B2C event venues (wedding venues, conference centers, event spaces). Actively testing new verticals. Requires strategy tooling that isn't SaaS-default in its assumptions. [E1]

## Decision Drivers

**"Does this compress my time-to-strategy enough to protect my guarantee?"** The 90-day clock is the primary filter. Any tool that doesn't visibly accelerate strategy delivery doesn't pass the bar, regardless of other features. [E1, E2]

**"Does it produce something I can actually send to a client?"** The output has to be agency-grade: grounded in real context, free from SaaS-default assumptions, structured enough to hand to a client or use as an execution brief. [E1]

**"Does it work for non-SaaS verticals?"** Event venues are not SaaS companies. Any tool with SaaS-default ICP, channel, or pricing assumptions is immediately disqualifying. The interview process has to surface the actual client context rather than projecting a default vertical. [E1]

**"Can I deploy this without the client needing to be technical?"** The agency owner uses Claude Code; the clients don't. The strategy artifacts need to be generated by the agency, not requiring client-side tool setup. [E1]
